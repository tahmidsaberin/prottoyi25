Trend Analysis

Topics Growing in Importance

  • Clippers & Clampers — 1–2 questions (2014–2017) → 3–5 questions (2020–2023). Now a mandatory numerical topic every year.
  • MOSFET — optional in early years, now appears every year with numerical circuit problems.
  • Logic Families (TTL/CMOS) — rare before 2021, now appears in every paper since 2021 with 6–8 marks allocated.
  • Digital Electronics — completely absent before 2024, now dominates Section A of 2024 paper. Structural shift.
  • Op-Amp numerics — growing in complexity. CMRR calculation, bandwidth, slew rate all increasing.

Topics Declining or Stabilized

  • SCR/DIAC/TRIAC — frequent in 2016–2021, absent or minimal in 2022–2024. Possibly being phased out.
  • Special Diodes — appeared as short notes frequently until 2021, now mostly single-line mentions.
  • Filters (capacitor/choke input) — appeared mainly in 2016–2017, not seen since.
  • Semiconductor Physics — still present every year but reduced to shorter 1–2 mark questions compared to 3-mark questions in 2014–2018.

Exactly Repeated Questions Across Years

  • JFET biasing circuit (IDSS=8mA, VP=-6V, 20V supply, Rd=3.3kΩ) — appeared with identical values in 2018 AND 2022. Very likely to repeat.
  • 741 op-amp noninverting amplifier problem (R1=1kΩ, Rf=10kΩ, A=200000) — appeared word-for-word in 2018 AND 2021.
  • Summing amplifier output (V1=50sin1000t mV, V2=10sin3000t mV, 33kΩ, 10kΩ, 330kΩ) — appeared identically in 2018 AND 2021.
  • CMRR differential amplifier problem — pattern of (voltage gain X, CMRR 90dB, two inputs + 1mV noise) appeared in 2016, 2017, and 2020.
  • Center-tap rectifier efficiency problem — appeared in 2016, 2017, 2020 with similar circuit values.
  • RTL/DTL/TTL comparison + CMOS NOR gate + CMOS XOR — appeared identically in 2022 AND 2024 Section B. High repeat probability.
  • CMOS inverter invert operation — appeared in 2021 AND 2022.

Pattern Shift: Theory → Applied Numerics

Early years (2014–2017) had more theory-heavy questions with circuit sketching. From 2020 onwards, questions increasingly require full numerical solutions — finding voltages, currents, biasing points, efficiency values. Even "explain" questions often now require a worked example. By 2024, almost every question is application-based.

Hidden Insights & Strategic Observations

  • Five questions have been repeated verbatim across different years: The JFET biasing circuit (2018 = 2022), the 741 op-amp noninverting problem (2018 = 2021), the summing amplifier problem (2018 = 2021), the RTL/CMOS block (2022 = 2024), and the CMRR numerical pattern (2016, 2017, 2020). Solve these from the original papers and you earn those marks guaranteed.
  • Clippers and clampers are chronically underestimated: Students focus on rectifiers but skip wave shaping. Yet clipper/clamper circuit problems have appeared in every paper since 2016 and carry 2–6 marks. They also require drawing skills — practice output waveforms by hand specifically.
  • The 2024 paper is a curriculum warning, not an anomaly: Digital circuit design (flip-flops, MUX, shift registers) was added to Section A in 2024 for the first time in the course's recorded history. This signals a permanent curriculum update. In 2025, expect these topics to be tested again — possibly with higher marks allocation.
  • The BJT biasing problem topology is always one of three types: Every biasing question in 10 years uses either (a) fixed bias, (b) voltage divider, or (c) collector feedback. Mastering these three topologies solves every possible biasing question in the course.
  • TTL vs CMOS comparison is guaranteed every year since 2021: Prepare the comparison table (power consumption, noise margin, speed, fan-out, supply voltage, cost) as a standalone flashcard. It returns in 2021, 2022, and 2024 with near-identical wording each time.
  • The JFET transfer curve sketch (given IDSS and VP) is an extremely high ROI question: It appears in 2017, 2018, 2019, 2022, and 2024. It always uses the same equation ID = IDSS(1 - VGS/VP)². Two minutes of practice per day for a week makes this a guaranteed 2–3 marks.
  • Section B op-amp questions reward methodology over memory: The CMRR and noninverting amplifier problems always provide all parameters. If you know the formulas and their derivations, you can solve any variation even unseen. Do not memorize specific answers — memorize the process.

Prioritized Study Roadmap

Phase 1 — Core (Days 1–5): ~65% of all exam marks

1
Rectifiers — Full-wave center-tap & bridge, efficiency derivation, ripple factor
Derive efficiency formula from scratch. Practice the center-tap circuit (5:1 transformer, 230V) until automatic. Know PIV for both types. Practice 3 different numerical problems from PYQs.
2
BJT Biasing — Fixed-bias, voltage-divider, collector-feedback + load line
Master the three circuit topologies. Practice finding IBQ, ICQ, VCEQ from scratch. Be able to draw the dc load line graphically AND verify algebraically. The exact same circuit appears every 2 years — solve from 2022 and 2024 PYQs.
3
Clippers & Clampers — All types, waveform sketching, DC level calculation
Draw output waveforms by hand for 5 different clipper and 3 clamper circuits from PYQs. Understand half-cycle analysis. This is consistently underestimated but carries 4–6 marks per paper.
4
JFET — Construction, transfer curve sketching, biasing numerical
Learn ID = IDSS(1 - VGS/VP)² cold. Practice sketching transfer curve given any IDSS and VP (takes 2 minutes). Solve the JFET biasing circuit problem from 2018 and 2022 (same values — memorize the approach).
5
BJT Configurations — CB/CE/CC, characteristics, α, β, transistor as switch/amplifier
Draw all three configurations from memory. Draw CE input and output characteristics correctly. Know the α–β relation derivation. Practice the CB circuit numerical (find IC and VCB given circuit values).

Phase 2 — Important (Days 6–8): ~25% of marks

6
MOSFET — Enhancement/depletion types, CMOS inverter, channel formation
Understand the channel formation mechanism for n-channel enhancement MOSFET. Know the CMOS inverter: how PMOS turns on when input is low, NMOS when input is high. Sketch transfer and drain characteristics.
7
Op-Amp — CMRR, summing amplifier, inverting/noninverting gain, slew rate
Memorize the CMRR dB formula: CMRR(dB) = 20log(Ad/Acm). Know inverting gain = -Rf/R1. Know noninverting gain = 1 + Rf/R1. Solve the exact 2018/2021 problems. Practice CMRR numerical from 2017 and 2020.
8
Logic Families + Semiconductor Physics — TTL vs CMOS, CMOS gates, energy bands
Prepare TTL vs CMOS comparison table (5 parameters). Know CMOS NOR gate circuit (2 NMOS + 2 PMOS). Know CMOS NAND gate. For semiconductors: energy band diagrams, doping, n/p-type are guaranteed marks for 10 minutes of reading each.

Phase 3 — Revision (Day 9–10)

9
Zener Diode · SCR/DIAC · Special Diodes · Digital Circuits (2024)
Zener network problem: 30 min. SCR V-I characteristics + DIAC construction: 30 min. LED, photodiode, solar cell: 20 min. Digital circuits (JK flip-flop, 4-to-1 MUX, shift registers): 2 hours — critical if 2025 follows 2024 trend.

Exam Day Strategy

  • Answer the rectifier efficiency + numerical question first — it is the most predictable and highest marks in Section A.
  • In Section B, prioritize BJT biasing (most predictable) and JFET biasing (repeated circuit values).
  • If a clipper/clamper numerical appears: draw the waveform step by step for each half-cycle — show your reasoning, partial credit is given for correct methodology.
  • For derivation questions (efficiency, ripple factor, α–β relation): write each step explicitly — examiners award marks for intermediate steps.
  • In questions with "draw and explain": draw first, label carefully, then write the explanation — the diagram carries more marks than prose.
  • You answer 3 of 4 questions per section — you have one skip. Use it on whichever of (SCR/DIAC, Special Diodes, Digital Circuits) you find weakest.
